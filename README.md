### User personas

Persona 1: The Burnt-Out High Achiever.

Name: Aarav Patel
Age: 17
Education: Grade 12(Science stream)
Setting: Hybrid education (school and coach apps).

Profile:
Aarav is an excellent student who is taking up competitive exams. His day is based on online classes and recorded lectures, test platforms and messaging groups. He sits before the screens 9-11 hours a day, most of which are academic.

Behaviors:

Between the late nights watches were used to record lectures.
Checks WhatsApp and Discord study groups all the time.
Works with productivity applications but feels lost in them.

Pain Points:

Constant eye stress and headaches.
Areas of mental exhaustion and loss of focus.
Screen use in the late night and disturbance of sleep.

Mental Health Impact:

High stress and anxiety
Burnout and emotional numbness symptoms.

Goals:

Balance academic life and health.
Enhance sleep patterns and concentration.

Needs:

Digital workload management tools.
Knowledge of healthy screening practices.
Regular interruptions and off-task learning.

The Social Media-Addicted College Student.

Name: Riya Sharma
Age: 20
Education: Bachelor (Arts and Humanities)
Setting: University living on campus.

Profile:
Riya relies on social media as the primary mode of interaction and identity. She spends Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube all day long and does not even notice that she spent so much time.

Behaviors:

Social Media Tweeting in classes.
Makes use of phone as soon as he wakes up and just before going to sleep.
Feels anxiety when disconnected.

Pain Points:

Poor concentration ability in academic activities.
Poor self esteem as a result of social comparison.
Sleepiness in spite of the little physical efforts.

Mental Health Impact:

Heightened stress and fluctuations.
Loneliness in spite of the constant Internet communication.

Goals:

Feeling self-sufficient and relaxed.
Lessen the reliance on social validation.

Needs:

Detection of addiction design patterns.
Digital well-being tools
Fostering face to face interaction.

Persona 3: Digital Fatigue Remote Learner.

Name: Kabir Mehta
Age: 22
Education: Final Year Engineering Student.
Setting: Completely distance education.

Profile:
Kabir receives online classes, develops codes and communicates through video calls. He hardly goes out of his room throughout the week and considers learning as being a matter of screens.

Behaviors:

Participates in several video calls per day.
Holds the camera, resulting in self-confidence.
Performs multiple tasks at lectures because of sleepiness.

Pain Points:

Digital overload due to being in cognitive overload.
Unaccounted physical exhaustion.
Deprivation of motivation and engagement.

Mental Health Impact:

Emotional detachment
Symptoms of mild depression

Goals:

Learn to feel motivated and have fun.
Reduce cognitive strain

Needs:

Break friendly learning models.
Other non-screen methods of study.
Normalization of mental health in learning institutions.

Persona 4: The Late-Night Gamer & Digital Escapist.

Name: Rohit Verma
Age: 18
Education: College Freshman.
Environment: Hostel / Home

Profile:
Gaming and online content are some of the ways through which Rohit avoids academic and social frustration. What began as a form of relaxation has now been a night-time habit that disrupts sleep and school work.

Behaviors:

Plays computer games late at night.
Watches hours of video stream and short content.
Absences morning classes because of exhaustion.

Pain Points:

Poor sleep quality
Poor academic achievements.
Irritability and low energy

Mental Health Impact:

More pressure and emotional reliance on digital escapism.
Increased fatigue and lack of resilience.

Goals:

Enhance sleep and education balance.
Minimize obsessive-compulsive consumption of digital behavior.

Needs:

Digital Interventions that focus on sleep.
Better coping mechanisms to stress.
Understanding of fatigue-addiction cycle.

Persona 5:The Digitally Overloaded yet Unconscious Student.

Name: Ananya Iyer
Age: 16
Tertiary Education: High School Pupil.
Environment: Smartphone-first learning.

Profile:
Ananya has been brought up in a digitally overrun world, and believes that being on the screen all the time is the norm. She does not know that her exhaustion and impatience can be associated with digital overload.

Behaviors:

Switching between learning applications and games.
Plays with phone during meals and homework.
Infrequently attends screen breaks.

Pain Points:

Persistent tiredness
Reduced attention span
Problem controlling emotions.

Mental Health Impact:

The initial symptoms of digital fatigue.
Greater level of distractibility and stress.

Goals:

Become more active and concentrated.
Striking a balance between online learning and well-being.

Needs:

Digital literacy education
Premature intervention and counseling.
Parental and Teacher support.
